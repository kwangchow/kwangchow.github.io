Sean Taylor - Award-Winning Interactive Producer

Hi, I'm a digital project director and strategic designer with twenty years planning, prototyping and producing interactive entertainment experiences across the web, mobile, television, console and social.

I help good people turn ideas into interactive experiences:

  • I helped Denki make the BAFTA-winning word game, Quarrel.
  • I helped Sophia George, V&A Designer in Residence, make Strawberry Thief, an experimental plaything based on the work of William Morris.
  • I helped Mind Candy make the hit music video kids app, Moshi Music.
  • I helped Turbulenz deliver gorgeous, console-quality, HTML5 browser games.
  • I helped Reagent Games plan and prototype their amazing new plaything.
  • I helped edtech startup, Systematic Learning produce a playable prototype of their pioneering EYFS playful phonics programme, Tap Tap Bat.
  • I helped DMA Design create the original Grand Theft Auto (as QA, my contribution was tiny, all credit to the team).

I've also written a book! Punk Playthings: Provocations for 21st Century Game Makers (CRC Press 2017) explores business model disruption, innovation and sustainability in modern game development.
